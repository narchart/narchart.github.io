Narrative Chart —— Let your charts tell stories with a few lines!

Narrative Chart is an open-source visualization library specialized for authoring charts that facilitete data storytelling with a high-level action-driven grammar. The library is implemented in JavaScript and compatible with most modern web browsers.

Highlights
Action-Driven Grammar

Narrative Chart use a novel action-driven grammar with a json format. Users can specify a chart design with a sequence of actions and Narrative Chart would parse the actions and execute them one by one. The grammar is intuitive and simple to use as it is close to real actions of designers, such as adding a title or modifying the chart on the canvas.

Storytelling Features

Common Chart Types

Narrative Chart support the creation of the charts frequently used in data storytelling, such as bar charts, line charts, pie charts, and unit visualization. We identified these commonly-used charts by coding thousands of Narrative Chart in the wild.

Annotation

Narrative Chart support 13 common annotating methods that aid the communication of data insights, such as highlighting a mark and adding a circle, symbol, and texture.

Title & Caption

Narrative Chart support the design of titles and captions, two elements that are prevalent in narrative visualization. Users can custominze the positions and styles (e.g., font-size, font-weight, border, background) of these two components.

Animation

Narrative Chart support the animation of all the visual components (e.g., title, caption, chart, annotation) to enhance user engagement with data and support the trannsition in data narratives.

Emotional Palette

Narrative Chart incorperate the emotional color palette from prior research and support 9 different color themes (e.g., playfulness, negativity, and trustworthiness) to help create an emotional storytelling atmosphere.

Embellishment

Narrative Chart support the creation of rich types of embellished charts (e.g., ISOTYPE charts, juxtaposing an image beside the chart) to increase user engagement and provide contextual information for data communication.
